About Me

Myspace.com. My introduction to HTML and CSS. At that point i had no idea a "web developer" was a thing. I spent hours moving images and boxes by a few pixels at a time to create pages that looked nothing like the myspace page it began life as.
When myspace changed its format and facebook began to dominate social media, my journey in coding dwindled. Over recent years i have struggled to figure out what was missing in my life and career, until i started looking at the html elements i hadn't seen for years. The problem solving aspect of web-development and meticulous nature of the job is what i was missing and i am so glad i found it again. After doing several free online courses i decided to bite the bullet and make the career change and train as a front-end web developer.
